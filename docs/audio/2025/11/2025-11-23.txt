Zooming out, the AI boom is becoming a significant economic force. Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell doesn't see AI spending as particularly sensitive to interest rates, suggesting it's driven by long-term investment in productivity. This massive spending is acting as a crucial support for the economy, helping to maintain growth.

Finally, the impact of AI is evident across various sectors. Companies like Caterpillar are seeing a surge in sales, particularly in equipment for data center applications. This highlights how AI is driving demand throughout the industrial supply chain, not just within the tech industry.

First up, the booming market is boosting profits and driving stock market highs, but it's not yet creating more jobs. Fed Chair Powell noted the labor market is still softening. While data centers offer short-term construction jobs, they don't need many staff once operational. Companies like Nvidia say they need more talent, but it's uncertain if this will balance out the rise in corporate layoffs.

Next, there's growing local opposition to building data centers across the country. Communities are concerned about their size, cost, and impact on utility prices due to high power consumption. While spending hasn't slowed yet, this could lead to reconsideration of where investments are made.

Meanwhile, the AI industry's race to build the future is far from over. Recent earnings show no finish line in sight. The industry is gearing up for a multimillion-dollar ad campaign through a new advocacy group called Build American AI, aiming to push for federal regulation over state laws.

Zooming out, Build American AI is a branch of the pro-AI super PAC, Leading the Future, which plans to invest heavily in the 2026 midterms. Unlike its parent, Build American AI is a nonprofit, allowing it to keep donor identities private. Leading the Future has already raised $100 million, setting it up as a major political player.

Zooming in, Build American AI will focus on advocating for federal regulation of AI, opposing state-level policies that could lead to overregulation. Some states, like California, have already enacted AI regulations. The group plans to spend significantly on advertising to influence this debate, though specific targets are not yet clear.

Finally, the leaders of Leading the Future emphasize their commitment to highlighting AI's benefits and countering misinformation. They aim to showcase the opportunities AI brings to workers and communities while challenging groups that they believe are hindering national leadership in AI.
