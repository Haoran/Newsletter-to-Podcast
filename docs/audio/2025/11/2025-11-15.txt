Next, let's talk about the ongoing AI boom. Spending in the AI sector is not slowing down; in fact, it's accelerating. Major players like Meta, Microsoft, and Google are all ramping up their investments. Meta plans to spend at least $70 billion on AI infrastructure this year, with even more next year. Meanwhile, Google is upping its forecast to at least $91 billion. Microsoft is also increasing its investments due to strong demand.

However, not everything is rosy. While tech giants are excited about the growth, there's a hint of caution. Some companies might be overextending themselves financially, creating a fragile situation where one weak link could cause issues.

Zooming out, the AI boom has become a significant economic force. Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell has dismissed concerns that lower interest rates could lead to an AI bubble. He believes the investments are driven by long-term productivity gains rather than interest rate fluctuations.

Finally, the ripple effects of AI spending are evident across industries. Take Caterpillar, for example. Their sales in power generation equipment have jumped 33%, largely due to the demand for data center engines. This shows that AI's impact extends beyond just tech companies, affecting the entire industrial supply chain.

First up, the economic boom is boosting company profits and stock market records, but it's not yet translating into more jobs. Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell noted that the labor market is still softening. While data centers create construction jobs, they don't need much staffing after they're built. Companies like Nvidia are still hiring, but rising corporate layoffs are a concern.

Next, there's growing local opposition to data center construction across the country. Communities are worried about their size, cost, and impact on utility prices due to high power consumption. This hasn't stopped spending yet, but it might influence future investment decisions.

Meanwhile, the AI industry is ramping up efforts to shape its future. A new group, Build American AI, is set to launch a multimillion-dollar ad campaign. Their goal? To push for federal regulation of AI, rather than a patchwork of state rules.

Zooming out, Build American AI is part of a larger pro-AI movement. It's a nonprofit, meaning it doesn't have to disclose its donors, unlike its sister organization, Leading the Future, which has raised $100 million for the 2026 midterm elections. This new group will focus on promoting the industry's legislative agenda.

Zooming in, Build American AI wants uniform federal regulations for AI, avoiding state-by-state differences. This aligns with former President Trump's stance. The group plans to spend big on advertising to influence political figures and counteract what they see as misinformation about AI's potential.

Finally, the leaders of Leading the Future emphasize the positive impact of AI on workers and communities. They aim to challenge groups they believe are spreading misinformation and hindering the nation's leadership in AI.
